The Cardiovascular System

The cardiovascular system is a closed circulatory system with a heart and branching network of arteries, capillaries, and veins. The cardiovascular system makes up the network that delivers blood to the body's tissues. It is the body's lifeline.

The cardiovascular system has three main functions. 1) It is responsible for transporting nutrients, oxygen, and hormones to cells throughout the body and the removal of metabolic wastes (carbon dioxide and nnitrogenous wastes). 2) It is responsible for the protection of the body by white blood cells, antibodies, and complement proteins that circulate in the blood and defend the body against foreign microbes and toxins. Clotting mechanisms are also present that protect the body from blood loss after injuries. 3) It regulates body temperature, fluid pH, and water content of cells.

The circulatory system is divided into three major parts: 1) the heart, 2) the blood, and 3) the blood vessels.

The heart is a muscle about the size of your fist. It pumps your blood and keeps the blood moving throughout your body. The blood is pumped by your heart. It travels through thousands of miles of blood vessels within your body It carries nutrients, water, oxygen, and waste products to and from your body cells. Blood is made up of liquids, solids, and small amounts of oxygen and carbon dioxide.

You have red blood cells, white blood cells (germinators), platelets, and plasma. Red blood cells carry oxygen and carbon dioxide. They pick up oxygen in the lungs and transport it to all the body cells. Then, it gets carbon dioxide and takes it back to the lungs, where we exhale and remove it. White blood cells fight germs. They attack and destroy germs when they enter the body. More white blood cells are produced when you have an infection. Platelets help stop bleeding. They stick to the opening of the damaged blood vessels. This attracts more platelets with help create a plug to stop the bleeding. They form scabs. Plasma is the liquid part of blood. It carries the blood cells and other components throughout the body. It is produced in the liver. Red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets are all produced in the bone marrow.

Arteries, capillaries, and veins are types of blood vessels. Arteries carry blood away from the heart, much of which is oxygen rich. Capillaries are tiny blood vessels. They connect arteries to veins. Nutrients, oxygen and waste pass in and out of your blood through the capillary walls. Veins carry blood back toward your heart.
